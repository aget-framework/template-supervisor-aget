# Critical Nature of Commitment Statements
*Scribbled: 2025-09-26*

## Recognition
When commitment statements ARE made (not aspirational, not directional, but actual commitments), they become critical contracts.

## Why This Matters
- **Trust Architecture**: Commitments build or destroy trust
- **Resource Allocation**: Real commitments require real resources
- **Accountability**: Commitments create obligations
- **Reputation**: Broken commitments damage credibility permanently

## The Gradient
1. **ASPIRATIONAL** - "Would be nice" (no commitment)
2. **DIRECTIONAL** - "Heading that way" (intent without promise)
3. **PLANNED** - "Intend to do" (soft commitment)
4. **COMMITTED** - "Will do" (hard commitment) ← CRITICAL
5. **GUARANTEED** - "Stake reputation on it" (existential commitment)

## Implications
- Never escalate to COMMITTED without resources secured
- ASPIRATIONAL is honest and sufficient for exploration
- Most missions should stay ASPIRATIONAL until proven
- Commitment elevation should be celebrated and rare

## Current State
The AGET-AGET mission is correctly labeled as ASPIRATIONAL. This is healthy - it acknowledges interest without creating false obligations.

When/if this changes to COMMITTED, everything changes.
